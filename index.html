Ayrton Senna

Fast and controversial but generous with those who were in need. The Formula One driver who gave a chance to its country's children

Here's a time line of Ayrton Senna's life:

  • 1960 - Born in Sao Paulo, Brazil
  • 1973 - Starts his first karting competition at the age of 13
  • 1977 - Wins South American Kart Championship
  • 1979 - Finishes second at the Karting World Championship
  • 1981 - Moves to Eaton, Norfolk. Wins the RAC and Townsend Thoresen Formula Ford 1600 Championships
  • 1982 - Wins European Formula Ford 2000
  • 1983 - Wins British Formula Three Championship
  • 1984 - Joins Toleman Motorsport and finishes his first Formula One season with 3 podiums and 13 points
  • 1985 - Joins Team Lotus and takes his first Grand Chelem (takes the Pole, win, fastest lap and leads all the laps of the race) and finishes 4th on the Championship
  • 1988 - Joins Mclaren Honda, takes 8 wins and his first F1 Championship
  • 1990 - Takes 6 wins and his second F1 Championship
  • 1991 - Takes 7 wins and becomes three times F1 World Champion
  • 1994 - Joins Williams Renault Team
    Dies at the age of 34 in a fatal crash at the San Marino Grand Prix.
